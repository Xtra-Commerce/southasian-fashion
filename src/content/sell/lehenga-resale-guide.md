## Introduction

Lehengas represent the highest-value category in South Asian fashion resale. The combination of three components (skirt, choli, dupatta), heavy embroidery, and celebratory occasions means lehengas command premium prices. However, selling lehengas successfully requires understanding their specific value drivers, specialized photography techniques, and shipping logistics that differ dramatically from single-piece garments.

This guide covers every aspect of turning your lehenga inventory into profitable sales.

## Understanding Lehenga Value Structure

### Lehenga Type and Price Positioning

| Type | Base Price Range | Primary Market | Value Retention |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Bridal/Heavy Embroidered** | $300-1,500+ | Weddings/major celebrations | 50-70% |
| **Semi-bridal/Party Wear** | $150-500 | Weddings, festivities, parties | 40-60% |
| **Cocktail/Festive** | $80-300 | Festivals, casual celebrations | 30-50% |
| **Daily/Casual Wear** | $30-120 | Daily use, informal events | 20-40% |

**Price multipliers by category:**

A heavy-embroidered lehenga in excellent condition retains **60-70%** of original purchase price. The same condition in casual wear retains only **30-40%**, because embroidery intensity directly correlates with occasion investment and longevity.

### Lehenga-Specific Value Drivers

Beyond standard Indian wear factors, lehengas have unique value markers:

**Skirt weight and fabric:**
- Heavyweight silk + zardozi = baseline +40%
- Lightweight silk or cotton base = baseline -20%
- Net skirt (party wear) = baseline -15%

**Choli construction:**
- Fully lined choli with structured boning = +20%
- Partially lined choli = +10%
- Unlined choli = baseline -10%

**Dupatta quality:**
- Heavy, complementary dupatta with embroidery match = +25%
- Lightweight dupatta (netted, simpler embroidery) = +10%
- Dupatta not included = -30%

**Three-piece completeness:**
- All three components present and matching = baseline
- Missing or mismatched dupatta = -25%
- Missing choli = -40%
- Only skirt (missing two components) = -60%

## Pricing Framework for Different Lehenga Types

### Bridal/Heavy Wedding Lehengas

**Characteristics:** 3-5kg total weight, extensive embroidery (zardozi, kundan, meenakari), $400-1,500+ original price.

**Resale pricing formula:**

```
Base Price = Original Purchase Price × 0.60 (60% retention)

Adjustments:
- Designer (established, e.g., Sabyasachi): × 1.5
- Rare color: × 1.1
- Regional significance: × 1.2
- Worn once: × 1.0 (if in pristine condition)
- Worn 2-3 times: × 0.85
- Altered for fit: × 0.85
- Minor staining/damage: × 0.70

Example:
Original Sabyasachi bridal lehenga: $1,200
Base: $1,200 × 0.60 = $720
Designer multiplier: $720 × 1.5 = $1,080
Worn once condition: $1,080 × 1.0 = $1,080
Final price: $1,080 (90% retention for designer)
```

**Pricing guardrails for bridal lehengas:**
- Never price below $200 (even heavily worn pieces hold this floor)
- Never price above original MSRP (scarcity alone doesn't override authenticity concerns)
- Heavy embroidery damage = reduce by $100-200 per significant loss
- Water/stain damage = reduce by $150-300

### Semi-Bridal/Party Wear Lehengas


## Photographing Lehengas: Technical Requirements

Lehengas require specialized photography because they're three-piece garments with multiple embroidered components. Poor photos leave 30-40% of potential buyers unconvinced.

### Essential Photo Set for Every Lehenga

**Minimum 12-15 photos per listing:**

1. **Full lehenga on hanger (front)** - Straight-on shot showing full skirt, choli, and dupatta drape. No angle. Shows proportions.

2. **Full lehenga on hanger (back)** - Reveals back choli design, back skirt embroidery, dupatta back detail.

3. **Full lehenga worn/on dress form** - On an actual person or mannequin, showing how it drapes and sits on the body. This single photo accounts for 40% of purchase confidence.

4. **Close-up: Choli detail (front)** - Macro shot of embroidery, embellishment attachment, fabric quality.

5. **Close-up: Choli detail (back)** - Back choli embroidery, construction, detail of closure.

6. **Close-up: Skirt border embroidery** - Shows complexity of border work, stone/thread quality, condition.

7. **Close-up: Skirt allover embroidery** - If embroidery covers skirt, show detailed section.

8. **Close-up: Dupatta detail (1)** - Embroidery/embellishment quality, corners if embroidered.

9. **Close-up: Dupatta detail (2)** - Opposite corner or different section showing design consistency.

10. **Choli seams and stitching** - Interior seam quality, durability markers, construction proof.

11. **Skirt hem and internal construction** - Hem finish, inner lining quality, condition of interior.

12. **Dupatta length demonstration** - Either on person showing drape or laid out straight with measurement reference (clothing inches, tape measure).

13. **Color reference shot** - Neutral background showing true color without filter. Include white or gray reference for accuracy.

14. **Wear/condition indicators** (if applicable) - Show minor wear, oxidation of zardozi, loose stitches (document these honestly).

15. **Components laid flat** - Optional but effective: all three pieces laid flat to show relationship and how complete set looks.

### Photography Setup for Lehengas

**Equipment needs:**
- Dress form or mannequin (essential; allows consistent, professional shots)
- White or light gray backdrop (5' × 8' minimum)
- Natural light from large window or outdoor space (no artificial light)
- Macro lens or close-up capability on smartphone (for detail shots)
- Tripod (consistency across shots)
- Measurement tape or ruler (for scale in macro shots)
- Iron or steamer (wrinkles destroy photos)

**Preparation before shooting:**
1. Dry clean or professionally press lehenga
2. Steam any remaining wrinkles
3. Arrange on dress form allowing 24 hours for wrinkles to release
4. Photograph within 48 hours of steaming

**Lighting technique for embroidery:**
- Position light to the side of embroidered sections (not directly above)
- This shows dimension and texture of embellishment
- Slight shadow makes stones/beads appear dimensional
- Avoid harsh direct light (washes out embroidery appearance)
- Overcast natural light is ideal

**Macro photography of embellishment:**
- Use actual macro lens (phone portrait mode insufficient)
- Zoom to 1:2 magnification minimum
- Show individual stone/bead attachment
- Ensure focus is sharp on embellishment, blurred background
- Photograph against dark background for clarity
- Take 2-3 versions; use clearest

### Common Photography Mistakes

**Don't photograph on person wearing it (if selling used):**
- Raises questions about wear and condition
- Distracts from garment details
- Limits buyer's ability to assess fit on their body

**Do photograph on dress form or hanger:**
- Professional appearance
- Clear visibility of construction
- Consistency across all photos

**Don't use filtered or edited photos:**
- Colors must match reality
- Any saturation adjustment kills trust
- Edited photos violate platform policies

**Don't group multiple lehengas in one photo:**
- Each piece needs individual documentation
- Buyers can't assess details with multiple pieces

**Don't photograph in inadequate lighting:**
- Embroidery must be clearly visible
- Underbelly lighting is essential
- Fluorescent light distorts color dramatically

## Shipping Lehengas Successfully

Lehengas present unique shipping challenges due to weight, fragility, and component protection.

### Packaging Materials Needed

- **Acid-free tissue paper** (3-4 sheets per component)
- **Unbleached muslin cloth** (for long-term wrapping)
- **Plastic hanger hooks** (to prevent tangling during shipping)
- **Cardboard clothing box** (minimum 16" × 12" × 4")
- **Bubble wrap** (only for fragile embellishments if needed)
- **Fragile labels** (for carrier identification)
- **Tissue paper** (standard white, for padding)

### Packing Procedure

**Step 1: Component preparation (24 hours before packing)**
- Dry clean or lightly steam if needed
- Allow 24 hours for wrinkles to fully release
- Ensure all components are completely dry (especially important for silk)

**Step 2: Packing the skirt**
- Lay clean tissue paper on flat surface
- Fold skirt lengthwise (careful not to crease embroidery)
- Wrap tissue around length (not too tight; allows circulation)
- Fold into thirds lengthwise if necessary to fit box
- Lay flat in bottom of box

**Step 3: Packing the choli**
- Wrap in tissue paper separately
- Stuff sleeves gently with tissue to maintain shape
- Place on top of skirt (not directly on embroidery)
- Alternate heavy/light components to balance weight

**Step 4: Packing the dupatta**
- Most fragile component if heavily embroidered
- Wrap in tissue paper loosely
- Fold into thirds
- Place on top, cushioned by tissue
- If heavily embroidered, consider separate layer of bubble wrap around edges

**Step 5: Box padding**
- Line box bottom with 2 inches of tissue paper
- Place all components
- Fill remaining space with tissue paper (provide cushion but not compression)
- Box should have some give when closed (not packed tight)

**Step 6: Sealing and labeling**
- Seal with brown packing tape (not masking tape)
- Label clearly: "FRAGILE - HANDLE WITH CARE" on top and side
- Include "Do Not Bend" labels if using non-standard box shape

### Shipping Method Selection

| Carrier | Transit Time | Cost | Protection |
|---------|---|---|---|
| **USPS Priority Mail** | 2-3 days (domestic) | $35-75 depending on weight | $100 basic |
| **UPS Ground** | 3-5 days | $40-85 depending on weight | $100 basic |
| **FedEx Ground** | 3-5 days | $45-90 depending on weight | $100 basic |
| **International (USPS)** | 2-4 weeks | $100-250 depending on country | $200-500 optional |
| **International (UPS)** | 3-7 days | $150-400 | $200-500 optional |

**Recommendation:** USPS Priority Mail for domestic (fastest, reasonable cost), include insurance for pieces over $300.

### International Shipping Considerations

**Customs declaration:**
- Declare accurate value (undervaluation creates delivery delays and customs issues)
- List as "Secondhand Indian Garment - Used"
- Include weight (critical for volumetric calculations)

**Additional time allocation:**
- Add 7-14 days to estimate for customs clearance
- Some countries delay Indian textiles for inspection
- Buyer should expect 3-4 week delivery for international

**Document what you send:**
- Take photo of packed box
- Keep tracking number
- Send tracking link to buyer immediately
- Keep packing photos for 30 days (dispute resolution)

## Managing Lehenga-Specific Returns

Lehengas have higher return rates than other garments (15-20% vs. 8-12% overall). Minimize these with policies:

### Return Policy for Lehengas

"If the lehenga arrives and condition does not match the listing description, returns are accepted within 14 days of receipt. Buyer pays return shipping (approximately $50-75 domestically). Upon receipt and inspection, I'll refund the full purchase price minus return shipping cost and a 5% restocking fee ($15-25 max).

Items returned must be in the same condition as received. If additional damage occurred in buyer's care, the return will not be accepted."

### Common Return Reasons and Prevention

**"Color doesn't match photos" (most common excuse for regret returns):**
- Prevent: Include color reference photos against white/gray backdrop
- Note any variations in listing ("slight variations between camera flash and natural light are visible")

**"Doesn't fit as expected":**
- Prevent: Provide detailed measurements; encourage buyer to measure against similar pieces
- Accept these returns; fit is subjective

**"Embroidery more worn than appeared in photos":**
- Prevent: Document any wear or oxidation with close-up photos
- Be honest about condition grade

**"Lehenga has strong odor":**
- Prevent: Indicate freshly dry-cleaned pieces; avoid musty storage odors
- Don't ship within 24 hours of dry cleaning (allows off-gassing)
