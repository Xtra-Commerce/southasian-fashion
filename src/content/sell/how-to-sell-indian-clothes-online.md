## Introduction

The secondhand Indian fashion market has exploded globally, with demand for authentic South Asian wear exceeding supply on most platforms. Whether you're clearing your closet or running a resale business, selling Indian clothes online requires understanding the unique characteristics of these garments—heavy embroidery, intricate construction, and cultural significance all factor into successful online sales.

This guide covers every step from choosing the right platform to optimizing listings that convert browsers into buyers.

## Step 2: Professional Photography That Sells

Indian clothes require specialized photography because draping, embroidery detail, and fabric weight aren't obvious in flat lays.

### Essential Photography Requirements

**Shoot 8-12 photos per listing:**

1. **Full garment on hanger** (straight shot) - Shows silhouette and length
2. **Full garment worn or on dress form** - Demonstrates how it actually sits
3. **Close-up of embroidery/details** (minimum 4 angles) - Buyers verify authenticity here
4. **Fabric texture shot** - Macro of weave, shows quality
5. **Hem and seams** - Condition verification
6. **Back view** - Many Indian pieces have distinct back designs
7. **Drape demonstration** (if relevant to piece type) - For sarees, lehengas, dupattas
8. **Scale shot with common object** - For jewelry, dupatta length verification

### Lighting Best Practices

- **Natural light only** - Window light shows true colors. Artificial light distorts silk, gold thread, and mirrors.
- **Avoid direct sunlight** - Creates harsh shadows on embroidery. Use diffused light.
- **White backdrop required** - Against walls or white sheets. Buyers need clean, professional images.
- **Time of day matters** - Shoot between 10 AM and 3 PM when light is consistent.

### Photography Mistakes That Kill Sales

**Don't:** Photograph in wearing the piece (buyer can't see detail and suspects poor condition)
**Do:** Use a dress form or professional hanger for every full-length shot

**Don't:** Use filtered or edited photos (colors must match reality)
**Do:** Use simple color correction only in editing—never alter saturation or tone

**Don't:** Group multiple items in one photo
**Do:** Individual product shots only

**Don't:** Photograph against colored backgrounds
**Do:** Neutral white or light gray always

## Step 3: Pricing Your Indian Clothes

Pricing South Asian wear is different from Western fashion because value factors are specific to the category.

### High-Value Indicators

- **Handloom textiles** (pure silk, cotton) = 30-40% higher baseline price
- **Named designer** (Sabyasachi, Tarun Tahiliani, Gaurav Gupta) = 2-4x retail
- **Heavy embroidery** (zardozi, kundan on silk) = add 20% per garment
- **Rare regional pieces** (authentic Paithani, Patola, Baluchari) = 50%+ markup over similar non-regional
- **Vintage/collector status** (pre-2000 handloom) = specialty pricing
- **Bridal wear** = premium category (1.5-3x price relative to similar casual piece)

### Pricing Formula

```
Base Price = Original Purchase Price × (100% - Age Discount - Wear Discount - Competition Discount)

Example:
Original Sabyasachi lehenga: $600
- Age discount (worn in 2023, so 3 years): -15%
- Wear discount (worn once): -20%
- Competition discount (10 similar items listed): -10%

$600 × 0.55 = $330 (starting price)
```

### Condition-Based Adjustments

| Condition | Price Retention | Requirements |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Never worn, tags attached** | 70-85% | Dust in original packaging only |
| **Worn once** | 55-70% | Must be pristine; no stains, odors, wear visible |
| **Lightly worn (2-5 times)** | 40-55% | Minor wear acceptable; no damage |
| **Moderately worn (6-20 times)** | 25-40% | Visible wear but no holes/stains |
| **Well-worn** | 10-25% | Suitable for crafters or heavy wear users |

**Pro tip:** Photos determine perceived condition. A lightly-worn piece photographed professionally might sell at "worn once" pricing. Poor photography drops condition perception by one full tier.

## Step 4: Writing Compelling Descriptions

Effective descriptions serve two audiences: search algorithms and human buyers. You must optimize for both.

### Description Structure

**Opening (2-3 sentences):** Immediate value proposition
- "Authentic Kanjivaram silk saree with traditional temple borders in rich burgundy. Handwoven by artisans in Kanchipuram, this piece combines heritage craftsmanship with versatile styling."

**Category Details (3-4 sentences):** Specific information
- "Saree length: 5.5 yards (standard drape length)"
- "Blouse piece included: 1 meter (unstitched)"
- "Fabric: 100% pure silk, hand-drawn border"
- "Condition: Worn once for ceremony, professionally cleaned"

**Embroidery/Embellishment Details:** This section commands premium pricing
- Identify embellishment type (zardozi, kundan, mirror work, chikankari, etc.)
- Location on garment ("Embroidery concentrated on pallu and border")
- Extent ("Heavy throughout" vs "Minimal accents")
- Materials ("Gold-plated zardozi thread, not costume gold")

**Measurements & Fit:**
```
Garment Measurements:
- Lehenga waist: 30"
- Lehenga length: 38"
- Choli bust: 32"
- Choli length: 14"
```

**Care Instructions:** Shows expertise and protects against returns
- "Dry clean only. Avoid direct sunlight when storing."
- "Do not machine wash or bleach."
- "Store in muslin cloth, not plastic (prevents moisture damage)."

**Shipping Notes:** Transparency builds trust
- "Ships folded in acid-free tissue and clothing box. Will arrive with minimal creases given fabric weight."
- "International shipping available; buyer covers customs."

### SEO-Optimized Keywords to Include

- Garment type (lehenga, saree, salwar kameez, dupatta)
- Occasion (wedding, party, festival, casual)
- Fabric (silk, cotton, chiffon, georgette, net)
- Embellishment (embroidered, beaded, sequined, zardozi)
- Origin/style (Banarasi, Kanjivaram, Paithani, Tussar)
- Color (rich jewel tones work better than "red")
- Size (30, S, Medium)

Example: "Banarasi silk saree with zardozi embroidery, gold paisley motifs, wedding wear, Indian traditional wear"

## Step 5: Optimizing Visibility

### Timing Your Listings

**Best times to list:**
- Wednesday-Friday (mid-week browsing)
- 7-9 PM (evening shopping sessions)
- 2-3 weeks before major festivals (Diwali, Holi, weddings season)

**Seasonal boosts:**
- Wedding season (March-May, September-December): +30-40% search volume
- Festival season (Diwali in October-November): +25% volume
- New Year (January): Wedding outfit searches peak

### Avoiding Listing Mistakes

**Don't use:**
- All caps (AUTHENTICATED BANARASI SILK) - looks desperate
- Excessive punctuation (!!!RARE!!!HANDWOVEN!!!) - appears unprofessional
- Comparison language ("Better than X brand") - can trigger platform flags
- Unverified claims (authentication without certificate)

**Do use:**
- Descriptive, clear titles with key info first
- Factual, measured language
- Specific, verifiable details
- Museum-quality photography

### Competitive Differentiation

**Offer value competitors don't:**
- Free professional cleaning before shipping
- Styling guide (how to wear, what to pair with, for what occasions)
- Fabric care certificate
- Extended returns (15-30 days vs standard 10)
- Video walkthrough of the piece (shows movement, drape, embroidery catch in light)

## Step 6: Handling Customer Communication

### Response Time Standards

- **1st message response:** Within 6 hours (platforms algorithm favors this)
- **Shipping updates:** Message immediately upon shipment
- **Issue resolution:** Within 24 hours

### Messaging Best Practices

**Example response to "Is this available?"**
"Yes, this Banarasi saree is currently available. Would you like me to hold it for 24 hours while you decide? I can also answer any specific questions about fit, condition, or care."

**Example response to size questions:**
"This choli has a 32" bust measurement and 14" length. For someone typically wearing US 0-2 or Indian XS, this would fit well. I'd recommend measuring your bust relaxed and comparing to these measurements."

**Handling returns:**
"I stand behind the condition of this piece. If it arrives and you feel the condition doesn't match the description, I'll issue a full refund within 7 days of receipt. Shipping costs are non-refundable, but I'll cover return shipping if I made an error."
